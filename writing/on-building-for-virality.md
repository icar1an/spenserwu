# on building for virality

This project originally started as some form of exploration into advertising technology, but that is awfully boring and I ended up getting very distracted by Instagram reels during this time. It was in my apartment — or one that I'm generously living out of. Thank you so much, Ashley, for making this possible.

Essentially, I realized that people will try to build a great product first and then do great marketing second. What if we completely flipped that thesis on its head and built something purely for marketing and then product second? If you just flip that on its end, what comes out? You naturally start thinking: what would look the absolute dumbest on Instagram reels?

I was thinking about that. I had this random fleeting thought from this guy named Aaron Kahn, who built this Arc'teryx detector, which just uses MediaPipe to detect if you're wearing Arc'teryx on your head. If so, it pulls up a quiz that asks you to name ten mountains. That's kind of funny and it's exactly what I was thinking about when you're building for virality first — honestly a complete waste of MediaPipe potential anyway.

The immediate idea that came to mind was: what if somebody was playing the Chrome Dino game with their head? The dinosaur is jumping, so you have to move your head up to control it every time — it would look so, so stupid. Ben didn't think this was possible. I thought he was completely wrong, so I ended up vibing out a very basic working version in half an hour. Naturally, if you can do the Chrome Dino game, what's the Chrome Dino game but with three other cardinal directions? Subway Surfers. You literally end up doing this in like two hours.

When we finished the entirety of the product, we thought it'd be hilarious if we just took it to West campus and did guerrilla marketing. You just find random people in the libraries and the various dorms and ask them to try playing with the product. The reception was insane because I don't think anybody has ever seen somebody else play Subway Surfers with their head. I ended up skipping class the next day to rapidly improve head mode and also add in automatic tracking with the head, perfecting sensitivity thresholds for gestures and hand-based commands. The way you perfect the technology was hilariously by watching a ton of short-form content and playing Subway Surfers. One of the ideas I talked about earlier was Subway Surfers — you just didn't think about Instagram Reels. Then you realize you could literally scroll Instagram Reels with your head and it's kind of perfect for that.

Ben got the thing into an auto-updating DMG, sped through the website, and we put it together in like 30 minutes. Cue a bunch of UI/UX improvements.

When you use a product a lot, you end up having this great intuition for what you should build next. This entire time I never once felt like I was the one actually in charge of the product, if that makes sense. The product literally made itself, and that's the most fun thing about building for a consumer when you are the consumer. You just know what should exist next.

I will say, fundamentally this is a MediaPipe wrapper. It's not a serious project — at this time, I hadn't fully conceived of the idea of Unserious Ventures. I fully intended on actually taking this a lot further, but after doing it I realized it stopped feeling as fun, and that's the irony of trying to take the equivalent of a shitpost idea seriously. There's zero, zero point in doing something like that.

The one interesting thing about building silly products is they're basically infinite content engines. You can do so much random stuff with it. You just go to lectures and scroll brainrot. There have probably been many people at Cornell who've seen me do this. I don't want my permanent legacy to be the guy who just scrolls Instagram reels using his head in lectures — I do way more than that.

A disgusting side use case of something like this is scrolling multiple reels at the same time, so you can pull up multiple devices. If I added iris tracking, you could just flick between them. Though I will say, doing this will definitely hurt your head, so do not recommend trying this at home.
